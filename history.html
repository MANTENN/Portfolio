10th-14th Century

Peak - covered 6 miles along the Mississippi River

Farmers - participants of a complex agricultural culture,"Mississipian"

Urban Cluster of 20K or 30K people in 13th Century

Complex Aggriculture termed as "Mississpian"

Had a Temple; huge pyramid - base of 15 acres and height of 100ft

Center of long distance Trading

Copper from Lake Superior, Mica from southern Appalachians & Conch shelss from the Atlantic Coast

Specialised craftsmen - known for high-quality flint hoes; exported through out the Missipi Valley

City closed with logs in 13th&14th century ~> violence

Similiar to other large cities in Eastern Hemisphere. The temple was built to show case the wealth and power of the city.
